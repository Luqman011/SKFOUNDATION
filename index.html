Why AIDS?

HIV is still claiming the lives of the youngs and the olds.

The majority of children living with HIV are infected via mother-to-child transmission (MTCT), during pregnancy, childbirth or breastfeeding. This is sometimes referred to as ‘vertical transmission’ or ‘parent-to-child-transmission’

Engage

Get tested and encourage your loved ones to do the same. Find an HIV testing here. Raise awareness. Find posters, images, and banners that you can download and share on social media here. Wear a red ribbon -- the internationally recognized symbol for AIDS awareness -- to show support and solidarity.

Save

There’s still a lot of work to be done. HIV disproportionately affects men who have unprotected sex with women, young adults, racial and ethnic minorities. 1 in 8 people don't know they're infected. Globally, about 36.7 million people are living with HIV (including 1.8 million children). About 25.5 million are in sub-Saharan Africa.

Stop

Discrimination against people with HIV is often rooted in a lack of understanding about the virus and how it spreads. Contact your local public health department to find community-based organizations that provide HIV/AIDS information, counseling, and testing.

People we help

SEO

A lot of children and the youth at large are facing the same challenges, so let's stand together for zero stigma and discrimination associated with HIV/AIDS.

Our Goals

1. Fighting discrimination and stigma associated with HIV/AIDS.
2. Prevention of HIV transmission in either testing or blood transfusion.
3. Creation of a HIV/AIDS patients communities to support their thoughts and behaviors.
4. Provision of afforadable HIV/AIDS treaments and testings.
